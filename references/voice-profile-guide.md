# Voice Profile Guide

Use when creating or applying a reusable voice profile.

## What to extract from samples

- overall feel
- sentence rhythm
- paragraph length
- openings
- endings
- directness
- warmth, humor, edge, restraint
- common rhetorical moves
- favorite transitions
- level of abstraction
- use of examples
- punctuation habits
- contractions/slang/formality
- words and phrases used often
- words and phrases avoided
- how disagreement is handled
- how uncertainty is handled

## Guardrails

- Do not parody the writer.
- Do not overfit surface quirks.
- Do not imitate living authors for deceptive purposes.
- Preserve the user’s actual intent over style mimicry.
- If samples are too few or too varied, label the profile tentative.

## Applying a profile

When applying a profile:
1. Identify the target genre and audience.
2. Adapt the voice to the genre rather than copying all habits blindly.
3. Preserve meaning.
4. Run a final pass for over-imitation and awkwardness.

## Save/reuse behavior

Do not save voice profiles to durable memory unless the user explicitly asks. If asked to save, ask where or use the project’s established memory/vault workflow.
